What is SCA3?

Spinocerebellar ataxia type 3 (also called Machado-Joseph disease) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder affecting balance, coordination, and movement. It's caused by a genetic mutation (expansion in the ATXN3 gene) that runs in families.

Key facts about Jeff's situation:

  • Early-stage with slight symptoms but very high function (SARA 1/40 is excellent)
  • Family history: father and brother both affected; onset typically age 49–52
  • Jeff's lower repeat count (65 vs. father's 72, brother's 70) may mean slower progression
  • No cure yet, but promising treatments in clinical trials and early-stage pipeline
  • Early enrollment in studies now may help optimize treatments when they arrive

Bile acid treatment prevents SCA3 movement problems in animals

April 17, 2026 · National Ataxia Foundation
Researchers in Portugal found that TUDCA (tauroursodeoxycholic acid)—a bile acid already approved by the FDA for gastrointestinal disorders—protected balance and coordination in mouse and worm models of SCA3. The drug works by stabilizing a protein called the glucocorticoid receptor (GR), preventing cell death in the brain and spinal cord.
What this means for Jeff:

TUDCA is far earlier-stage than VO-659, but it's encouraging because it's an FDA-approved drug (faster path to trials than new compounds). No human trials yet—more animal safety work needed first. Watch this space in 2027+. GR levels in blood may also become a biomarker for tracking SCA3 progression.

Most advanced SCA3 therapy — VO-659 (Vico Therapeutics)

Phase 1/2a ongoing
ASO (antisense oligonucleotide) therapy. Twice-annual dosing began Feb 2026. FDA IND cleared for US trials. In Huntington's disease cohort, achieved 38% reduction in mutant huntingtin protein in cerebrospinal fluid.
What this means for Jeff:

Realistic near-term opportunity. US trial enrollment expected late 2026. CRC-SCA enrollment at Northwestern may provide pathway to access. Dr. Alfradique-Dunham will discuss site availability at May 26 visit.

CRD-002 (Cure Rare Disease)

Legitimate but earlier-stage
Gene therapy approach. Non-profit funded by CIRM grant. IND submission to FDA expected 2026. Scientifically sound but ~5–8 years behind VO-659 in realistic patient access.
What this means for Jeff:

Worth monitoring but not an immediate option. Will track progress in October 2026 pipeline review.
